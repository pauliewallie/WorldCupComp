World Cup Prediction Competition

Welcome to this charity competition running during the 2014 FIFA World Cup. How does it work? You enter a score prediction for each game and get points for predicting the correct scores. There are cash prizes for the overall top players. Also, you can create a mini competition and see who comes out on top between your group of friends.
It costs only €5 to enter and 100% of the proceeds go to Laura Lynn Children's Hospice

Prizes: 1st Place €500     2nd Place €200     3rd Place €100

Competition Rules

  • Players make a prediction on each fixture in the Fifa World Cup by predicting the score in the game and/or predicting the correct result
  • Players receive 1 point for predicting the correct game winner or a draw
  • Players receice 2 points for each team's score predicted correctly
  • 5 points in total are available for each game if you get the correct score of both teams
  • The predictions must be made before the game starts but you can make predictions as much in advance as you wish
  • Predictions are for normal time only i.e. the result and score after 90mins
  • Players can enter predictions for all or only some games. Obviously, more predictions gives you a better chance
  • The cost of entry is a minimum of 5 euro. More will be gratefully accepted since this is for charity
  • Winners will be asked to produce their receipt of payment

Terms & Conditions

  • This is a "not for profit" competition with all the proceeds going to charity. No expenses will be taken for set-up, administration or prizes
  • All prizes have been kindly donated by the sponsors
  • A permit for the competition has been received from An Garda Siochana
  • Winners will be contacted and published on this website within 7 days of the World Cup Final on 13th July 2014
  • The competition organisers decision is final
  • The competition organisers reserve the right to cancel the competition if participation is below viable levels. In this case, all donations will be refunded in full
  • In the event of a tie, the prize money will be equally split
